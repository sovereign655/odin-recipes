Savory Elegance on a Budget - The $2 Steak



Introduction:

In this culinary endeavor, we embark on a delightful journey to create a delectable $2 steak that rivals the finest cuts. Prepare to be astonished as we balance artistry and frugality in perfect harmony. Our ingredients may be humble, but with the expert guidance of this Gordon Ramsay-inspired recipe, we shall elevate the mundane into a symphony of flavors fit for royalty.


Ingredients:


  • A well-marbled, budget-friendly steak (e.g., chuck or sirloin, approximately 8-10 oz)
  • Coarse sea salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Unsalted butter
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh thyme sprigs
  • Garlic cloves (peeled and crushed)
  • Shallot (finely diced)
  • A splash of balsamic vinegar
  • Optional: A sprinkle of red pepper flakes (for a hint of heat)


steps:


  1. Prepare the Steak: Remove the steak from the refrigerator and let it sit at room temperature for approximately 30 minutes, allowing it to relax and ensuring even cooking.

  2. Seasoning the Star: Gently pat the steak dry with paper towels to encourage a beautiful sear. Season both sides generously with coarse sea salt and freshly ground black pepper, ensuring each grain of salt and speck of pepper adheres to the meat.

  3. The Pan Dance: In a heavy-bottomed skillet, heat a drizzle of olive oil over medium-high heat until it shimmers. Add the seasoned steak to the pan, allowing it to sizzle enchantingly as it meets the hot surface. Allow the steak to cook undisturbed for 3-4 minutes, forming a glorious caramelized crust on one side.

  4. The Magic Flip: Carefully turn the steak using a pair of tongs, treating it with the utmost respect. Add a couple of tablespoons of unsalted butter to the pan, along with the fresh thyme sprigs and crushed garlic cloves, allowing the flavors to weave their way into the steak.

  5. Infuse the Symphony: Baste the steak with the melted butter using a spoon, ensuring every nook and cranny is touched with the herbaceous and garlicky goodness. Optional: For a subtle fiery twist, sprinkle a pinch of red pepper flakes over the steak.

  6. The Moment of Rest: Remove the steak from the refrigerator and let it sit at room temperature for approximately 30 minutes, allowing it to relax and ensuring even cooking.

  7. A Symphony of Aromas: In the same pan, sauté the finely diced shallots until they turn soft and translucent, absorbing the residual flavors of the steak. De-glaze the pan with a splash of balsamic vinegar, scraping up any delightful browned bits to create a luscious sauce.

  8. The Finale: Drizzle the tantalizing balsamic reduction over the rested steak, enveloping it in a symphony of flavors. Adorn the steak with a sprig of fresh thyme, symbolizing the elegance and simplicity of this masterpiece.


Conclusion:


Behold, the magnificence of our $2 steak! A tantalizing triumph born from budgetary constraints and culinary prowess, worthy of applause in any fine dining establishment. As Gordon Ramsay would say, "Bon appétit!"
